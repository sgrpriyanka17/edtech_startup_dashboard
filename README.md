**1.Ed Tech Online Courses Insights Dashboard**

A dynamic,interactive data visualisation tool built to explore around 10,000 online courses from popular online learning platforms like Coursera, Udacity, Simplilearn, and FutureLearn including information about their course type, language, skills, instructor, rating, duration and number of viewers.

**2.Description**

This dashboard transforms raw, multi-dimensional educational data into actionable insights through intuitive visual storytelling, enabling users to understand trends through platform comparison.Users can evaluate how different providers perform across key metrics.The dashboard helps in evaluating course effectiveness by correlating multiple indicators such as ratings, enrollments, and duration.

**3. Tech Stack**

The dashboard was built using the following tools and techniques:

_Power BI Desktop_- Main data visualisation platform used for report creation. 

_Power Query_ Data transformation and cleaning layer for reshaping and preparing the data. 

_DAX(Data Analysis Expressions)_- Used for calculated measures, dynamic visuals, and continual logic.

_Data Modeling_ -Relationship established among tables to enable cross-filtering and aggregation. 

_File Format_-.pbix for development and .png fro dashboard previews. 

**4.Data Source**- https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/khaledatef1/online-courses

**5.Problem Statement**

With the rapid growth of online learning platforms such as Coursera, Udacity, Simplilearn, and FutureLearn, a vast number of courses are available across diverse domains, skill levels, and languages. However, this abundance of information creates challenges for learners and stakeholders in effectively identifying relevant, high-quality, and suitable courses.

**6.Key questions**

Which platform offers the highest number of courses?

Which platform attracts the most learners based on enrollments/views?

What types of courses are most commonly offered?

Which languages are most commonly used for course delivery?

What are the most in-demand skills and subject areas?

Which domains (e.g., Data Science, Business, Programming) dominate the course landscape?

Which courses have the highest ratings?

Who are the top instructors based on ratings and enrollments?

What course duration is most preferred by learners?

Which courses should a learner choose based on their skill level, time availability, and language preference?

**7.Goal of the dashboard**

The goal of this Power BI dashboard is to provide a unified, interactive platform for analyzing and exploring large-scale online course data from leading e-learning providers.It aims to transform complex and scattered educational data into meaningful, easy-to-understand insights that support users in making informed decisions about learning and skill development.

**8.Key visuals**

_a.Course type vs Count of course Id(Stacked Bar graph)_

Showing:Course with highest number of offerings,market demand trends or content strategy differences

For Learners: Helps identify whether enough courses exist at their skill level

For Platforms: Highlights opportunities to create content in underrepresented levels

For Analysts: Reveals learning trends and audience targeting strategies

_b.Average of number of viewers vs subtitles language count(line chart)_

Showing:Whether adding more subtitle languages increases course reach,how accessibility impacts learner engagement,the effectiveness of multilingual support across platforms 

_c.Average of Number of viewers vs subcategory and language (Stacked Column chart)_

Showing:viewer engagement across different subject areas,language influences popularity within each subcategory,content performance across platforms

_d.Category and Rank category by Average Views(Matrix)_

Showing:Top-performing categories based on engagement,Rank categories from most to least popular


_e.Instructor rating vs Instructors(Clustered Column chart)_

Showing:instructor performance based on course ratings,top-rated instructors,variation in teaching quality across instructors


_f.Average of Number of viewers vs Duration in hours(Line Chart)_

Showing:whether shorter or longer courses attract more viewers,optimal course duration for maximum engagement, learner preferences across platforms 

_g.Category , Average of count of skills provided and Average of Duration in hours(Matrix)_

Showing:learning depth (skills) across categories,time commitment required for each category

**9.Business impact and insights**
_a.Improved Decision making for Learners:_Helps users identify high-rated and popular courses quickly and enables selection based on skill level, duration, and language preference which reduces time spent searching through thousands of courses.

_b.Strategic Insights for Platforms:_Identifies high-demand categories and trending skills that supports data-driven decisions for expanding into new domains or languages.

_c.Enhanced Content Optimization:_Reveals the relationship between course duration and engagement,helping design optimal course lengths.
It shows how subtitle languages impact viewership, encouraging multilingual accessibility.

_d.Market Expansion Opportunities:_Language analysis uncovers opportunities to target global audiences and encourages platforms to invest in localized and inclusive content.

_e.Instructor Performance Evaluation:_Highlights top-performing instructors based on ratings and engagement which helps platforms retain high-quality instructors and improve underperforming ones.

_f.English as the Primary Language:_A large proportion of courses are offered in English, showing limited multilingual diversity.

_g.Certain Categories Drive Maximum Engagement:_Domains like Data Science, Business, and Programming tend to have higher average viewers, indicating strong demand.
